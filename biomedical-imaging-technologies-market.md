<p><p>Biomedical imaging technologies refer to various imaging techniques used in the medical field to visualize the internal structures of the body. Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) uses magnetic fields and radio waves to create detailed images. Computed Tomography (CT) imaging produces cross-sectional images by using X-rays and computer processing. Nuclear imaging involves the use of radioactive materials to produce images of internal organs or tissues. Ultrasonic imaging uses high-frequency sound waves to generate images. There are also other imaging technologies available, such as optical coherence tomography and positron emission tomography, which aid in diagnosing and monitoring diseases.</p></p>
