<p><p>Anode materials for sodium-ion batteries can be classified into two main types: hard carbon and soft carbon. Hard carbon anodes are typically made from materials like petroleum coke and pitch coke, which have a high capacity for storing sodium ions. Soft carbon anodes, on the other hand, are usually derived from biomass or polymers, offering a lower capacity but higher rate capability. Both types of anode materials have their unique advantages and are used in different applications based on their specific characteristics.</p></p>
