<p><p>Watercolor paints can be categorized into different market types based on their characteristics. Transparent watercolors offer a translucent effect when applied, allowing light to pass through the paint layer to reflect off the white paper below. Opaque watercolors, on the other hand, have a more solid and opaque finish. Other watercolor paint variations may include specialty paints like metallic or iridescent options, as well as gouache, which is similar to opaque watercolor but with a heavier pigment concentration. Each type offers different effects and advantages for artists.</p></p>
