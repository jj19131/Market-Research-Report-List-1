<p><p>Electricity trading market types can be broadly categorized into pit trading and over-the-counter (OTC) market. In pit trading, physical exchanges take place in a centralized location, often referred to as a trading pit or floor, where traders meet to buy and sell electricity contracts. This method offers face-to-face interaction and immediate settlement. On the other hand, the OTC market involves direct negotiation between buyers and sellers, typically facilitated by brokers or electronic platforms. This market type provides more flexibility and privacy as trades are not bound by standardized contracts or exchange rules.</p></p>
