<p><p>Titanium Dioxide (TiO2) is a white pigment used in various industries. It is produced using two main processes - Sulfate Process and Chloride Process. The Sulfate Process is the most widely used method, accounting for a large portion of the market. This process involves reacting titanium minerals with sulfuric acid. On the other hand, the Chloride Process is known for producing high-quality TiO2 with lower impurities, but it is more expensive and less common in comparison to the Sulfate Process.</p></p>
