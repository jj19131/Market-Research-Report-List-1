<p><p>In the Cross-Laminated Timber (CLT) market, there are two main types: adhesive-bonded CLT and mechanically fastened CLT. Adhesive-bonded CLT involves the use of a strong adhesive to bond the layers of timber together, creating a solid and durable product. Mechanically fastened CLT, on the other hand, utilizes mechanical fasteners such as screws or nails to hold the layers of timber together. Both types offer structural and environmental advantages, and their choice depends on factors like project requirements, cost, and personal preference.</p></p>
