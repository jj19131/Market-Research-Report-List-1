<p><p>Medical insulin pumps are devices used by people with diabetes to administer insulin on a continuous basis. Patch pumps are a type of insulin pump that adheres to the skin and delivers insulin through a cannula. Normal pumps are traditional insulin pumps with tubing that connect to the body. Patch pumps are gaining popularity due to their convenience and discreet nature, while normal pumps offer more control and customization options. Both types of pumps play a crucial role in managing diabetes and improving quality of life for patients.</p></p>

<p><p>Medical insulin pumps are used in both Type I and Type II diabetes markets to provide constant insulin delivery to regulate blood sugar levels.
