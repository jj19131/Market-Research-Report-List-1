<p><p>Seizure treatments encompass various approaches, such as anticonvulsant therapy and surgical intervention. Anticonvulsant therapy involves the use of medications to control, prevent, or reduce the frequency of seizures. These drugs work by suppressing abnormal electrical activity in the brain. On the other hand, surgery may be an option for individuals with severe, drug-resistant seizures. Surgical procedures aim to remove the brain area responsible for generating seizures or to implant stimulators that can modulate brain activity. Both anticonvulsant therapy and surgery strive to alleviate seizures and improve the quality of life for patients.</p></p>
