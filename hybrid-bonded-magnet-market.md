<p><p>Hybrid bonded magnets are classified into four main types based on the manufacturing process: Compression Process, Extrusion Process, Calendering Process, and Other markets. The Compression Process involves shaping the magnet material under pressure, while the Extrusion Process involves forcing the material through a die to create a specific shape. The Calendering Process involves rolling the material into a thin sheet. Other markets refer to any additional methods used in the production of hybrid bonded magnets beyond the three main processes.</p></p>
