<p><p>The animal rabies vaccine market includes two types of vaccines: injectable vaccine and oral vaccine. Injectable vaccines are administered through injections, typically by trained professionals. They provide protection against rabies by stimulating the animal's immune system to produce antibodies. On the other hand, oral vaccines are administered through baits or bait-like devices, making them easier to distribute in wildlife populations. Once consumed, the oral vaccine triggers an immune response, offering protection against rabies. Both types of vaccines play essential roles in preventing the spread of rabies among animals and reducing the risk to humans.</p></p>
