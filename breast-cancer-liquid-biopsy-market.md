These include Circulating Tumor Cells (CTCs), which are cancer cells that have detached from the tumor and entered the bloodstream. Cell-Free DNA (CfDNA) refers to small fragments of DNA released by cancer cells that can be found in the blood. Extracellular Vesicles (EVs) are tiny structures released by cancer cells containing important biological materials. Other circulating biomarkers are substances found in the blood that can indicate the presence of breast cancer.</p></p>
