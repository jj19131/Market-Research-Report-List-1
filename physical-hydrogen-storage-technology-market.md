<p><ul><li>High Pressure Gaseous Hydrogen Storage</li><li>Low Temperature Liquefied Hydrogen Storage</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Physical hydrogen storage technology includes two main types - high pressure gaseous hydrogen storage and low temperature liquefied hydrogen storage. In high pressure gaseous storage, hydrogen gas is stored at extremely high pressures in tanks, making it compact but requiring reinforced infrastructure. Low temperature liquefied storage involves cooling hydrogen gas to cryogenic temperatures, turning it into a liquid for storage in insulated tanks. While both methods have their own benefits and challenges, they play a crucial role in the advancement of hydrogen as a clean energy source.</p></p>
