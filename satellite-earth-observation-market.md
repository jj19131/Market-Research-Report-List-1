<p><p>The Satellite Earth Observation market comprises three types based on the orbital distance: Low Earth Orbit (LEO), Medium Earth Orbit (MEO), and Geosynchronous Earth Orbit (GEO). LEO satellites, located closer to Earth, provide high-resolution data in real-time but have limited coverage. MEO satellites, positioned further away, offer extended coverage and moderate resolution. GEO satellites, positioned at fixed points above the Equator, provide continuous coverage of specific areas but have lower resolution. These different market types cater to diverse requirements such as emergency response, weather forecasting, and environmental monitoring.</p></p>
